Welcome to Lahore

Discover the cultural heart of Pakistan, where majestic Mughal architecture seamlessly blends with vibrant modern heritage, lush historic gardens, and an unparalleled culinary legacy.

1. The Majestic Badshahi Mosque

Commissioned by the Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb in 1671, the Badshahi Mosque stands as an iconic monument of Islamic architecture. Built entirely with red sandstone and decorated with intricate white marble inlay, its vast courtyard can accommodate over 100,000 worshippers. The mosque's grand minarets dominate the old city's skyline, offering a profound glimpse into Pakistan's rich historical narrative.

2. The Historic Lahore Fort (Shahi Qila)

Situated directly opposite the Badshahi Mosque, the Lahore Fort is a magnificent citadel that encapsulates centuries of royal history. Successive Mughal, Sikh, and British rulers left their architectural mark on this UNESCO World Heritage site. Notable highlights include the shimmering Sheesh Mahal (Palace of Mirrors), the grand Diwan-i-Aam, and the stunningly decorated Picture Wall showcasing complex tile mosaics.

3. The Royal Shalimar Gardens

Constructed by Emperor Shah Jahan in 1642, the Shalimar Gardens are a masterclass in classic Mughal charbagh layout. Spread across three descending tiers or terraces, the gardens feature hundreds of continuous fountains, white marble pavilions, and highly curated water cascades. This architectural oasis was intentionally crafted to symbolize an earthly paradise, highlighting historic engineering marvels of hydrological design.
